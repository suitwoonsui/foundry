# Pillars, paths, and the funding page

**Seven Pillars of the Foundry** is the umbrella: seven areas where the community holds the project up. Within each pillar, people progress on a **path** of earned recognition (Heralds, Council, Orators, Diplomats, Forge, Vault, and **The Hearth** for everyone who lives in what we ship). This doc covers culture, recognition levels, where funders and product users fit, and the funding page as entry gate. Previous: [01-core-concept-wallets-and-layout.md](01-core-concept-wallets-and-layout.md). Next: [03-path-naming-options.md](03-path-naming-options.md).

So instead of pay-to-rank tiers, we use **Seven Pillars of the Foundry** as the high-level frame, and **paths** for how someone actually contributes—or **uses what we ship**—and levels up. Each pillar is a domain (social, strategy, voice, partnerships, build, fuel, **home / product**). Each named path—The Heralds, The Council, The Orators, The Diplomats, The Forge, The Vault, **The Hearth**—is how you show up inside that pillar, with earned recognition levels along the way.
This lets people belong to something that fits who they already are.

**Application users / players / tool users**
**The Hearth** (see [03-path-naming-options.md](03-path-naming-options.md) for alternate path names)
The home lane: plays the games, runs the tools, connects wallets, comes back—**participation through the product itself**. Ranks: **Visitor** → **Resident** → **Fixture** (from crossing the threshold, to putting down roots, to becoming indispensable to the rhythm of the place).

**Why a seventh pillar:** the other six paths describe how people **push** the ecosystem (voice, build, fund, etc.). None of them is “I’m here because I **use** what you built.” That person is not a lesser participant; they are how the work proves out. **The Hearth** makes room for recognition that is **not** pay-to-rank and **not** forced into Forge or Heralds.

These are not ranks — they are identities.
A person chooses their path based on who they actually are.
And people can walk more than one path if they contribute in multiple styles (e.g. Vault + Hearth).
This encourages authentic contribution, not grinding or posturing.

So where do funders fit?
Funding is one path (The Vault), equal to others.
This prevents:
Elites


Whales controlling direction


Pay-to-win culture


Resentment between non-funders and funders


Someone who funds is simply someone whose contribution form is fuel, not time.
They do not have more governance power.
They do not outrank others by default.
They are respected as one contributor type among many.
Perfect alignment with:
Team drives. Community fuels.

Now, the funding page fits in naturally
The funding page is not "please donate."
It is:
Choose how you fuel the engine.
Where the contribution options include:
Raid campaigns (earn)


Build tools or content (create)


Host AMAs or bring partners (connect)


Or fund a milestone (fuel)


**Use our apps** (show up in product—the Hearth path)


This page becomes the entry gate to choosing a path.
Someone who contributes funding is on the Vault path, just as someone who raids progresses on the Heralds path—and someone who **lives in the product** progresses on the Hearth path.
No hierarchy.
Just different forms of strength.
